Bug: Weight Integration Tests Fail — Explained Simply
======================================================

Imagine a bank with multiple tellers (windows).

THE SETUP:
- The database is the bank vault where all the money is stored.
- An "app context" is a teller window. Each window has its own desk,
  its own notepad, and its own conversation with the vault.
- A "session" is the notepad the teller uses to track what they're doing.
  When the teller finishes and closes the window, the notepad is thrown away.

WHAT THE TESTS ARE TRYING TO DO:

  1. A manager (setup_and_cleanup) opens Window A.
     She writes "add test containers" on her notepad,
     walks to the vault, stores them, comes back, and CLOSES Window A.
     Her notepad is thrown away.

  2. A customer (the test) opens Window B.
     He gets a fresh notepad. He says:
     "Deposit 15000kg for truck TEST-GW-01" (this is the POST).
     The teller writes it on the notepad, walks to the vault, stores it.
     Then the customer says:
     "Show me all transactions from today" (this is the GET).

THE PROBLEM:

  Window B has its own notepad (session). Even though the deposit
  was stored in the vault, the teller at Window B might be looking
  at a SNAPSHOT of the vault from when she first sat down — before
  the deposit happened. She doesn't see the new deposit because her
  notepad says "nothing new here" based on her old snapshot.

  This is called "transaction isolation" — the database protects
  each teller from seeing half-finished work from other tellers.
  But in this case, it's protecting the GET from seeing the POST's
  data, even though they're part of the same test.

WHY SOME TESTS PASS AND SOME FAIL:

  Think of it this way: some tests ask broad questions like
  "show me ANY deposit from today" — and they find old leftover
  data from other tellers that happened to be visible. They pass
  by accident.

  Other tests ask specific questions like "show me the deposit
  for EXACTLY 15000kg of oranges" — and that specific deposit
  was made by a teller whose work isn't visible yet. They fail.

THE FIX:

  Stop using multiple windows. Put everything — the setup, the
  deposit, the query, and the cleanup — at ONE window, with ONE
  notepad. That way the teller sees everything she wrote down,
  because it's all on the same notepad.

  In code terms: merge the two fixtures (setup_and_cleanup + client)
  into a single fixture that uses one app context. Everything
  shares one session, one view of the database, no isolation
  surprises.
